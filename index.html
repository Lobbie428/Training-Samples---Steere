Overview

Philosophy

"Everybody is a genius. But If you judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree, it will live its whole life believing that it is stupid"
When it comes to training knowing your audience is paramount. Content mastery and presentation skills and strategy follow.

Understand the audience point of view so you're not shooting-in-the-dark when delivering content.

Master content so you can tailor curriculum to add maximum value to the audience.

Present content in a way that engages the audience and be flexible as an instructor by reducing friction and easeing trainees into learning.

Training Strategy

Designing, developing and delivering training content is an iterative learning process. Knowing the concepts and the audience and presenting content well is followed by refinement of that content as software evolves and feedback is collected from trainees.

Instructional Design methods like the ADDIE and Backward Design frameworks are concepts I apply everyday as I develop and maintain content.

Applying principles of andragogy, or the methods and practice of teaching adult learners, helps ensure content is engaging and well received by the software experts I typically train.
